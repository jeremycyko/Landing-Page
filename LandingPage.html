The lion does not concern himself with being overworked. He calls it momentum.
Lai.en (Noun): a large, social feline predator native to Africa and India, recognized by its tawny coat, tufted tail, and a thick mane on males.
As apex predators living in prides, they hunt prey like wildebeest and zebra. They are frequently used as symbols of strength and courage.
Four Ways To Think Like a Lion
A lion moves with quiet confidence, never rushing but always in control of its territory.
A lion chooses its battles wisely, conserving energy until the moment demands decisive action.
A lion trusts its instincts, acting with clarity instead of hesitation or doubt.
A lion leads with presence alone, commanding respect without needing to prove its strength.
